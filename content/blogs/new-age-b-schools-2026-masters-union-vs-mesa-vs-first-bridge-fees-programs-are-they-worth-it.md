# New-Age B-Schools 2026: Masters' Union vs Mesa vs First Bridge — Fees, Programs & Are They Worth It?

* - Three new-age B-schools disrupting India's MBA landscape — each with a distinct identity and bet on what "business education" should mean in 2026
* - Masters' Union (Gurugram) bets on Tech + Business, with avg. placements of ₹28.52 LPA
* - Mesa (Bangalore) bets on entrepreneurship and startup leadership, with PGP fees at ₹17.45 lakh
* - First Bridge (Gurugram) bets on AI-integrated, job-ready skills with 80+ industry partners

## The New MBA Conversation

India's B-school landscape has a new problem — and it's not too few options. It's too many options claiming to be "different."

Every new-age B-school today promises industry immersion, smaller cohorts, real-world faculty, and placement-first thinking. The brochures look great. But with fees ranging from ₹17 lakh to ₹50+ lakh, the stakes are too high to pick a school based on a slick Instagram reel.

Masters' Union, Mesa School of Business, and First Bridge are three of the most talked-about names in this space right now. They're genuinely trying to rethink business education. But they're not identical — and the wrong pick for your profile could be an expensive mistake.

Here's the unbiased breakdown.

## Masters' Union: Where Tech Meets Business

**Location:** Gurugram, Haryana
**Focus:** Technology + Business Management
**Founded by:** Pratham Mittal (former VP, LinkedIn Learning)

Masters' Union launched with a clear thesis: the future belongs to people who can operate at the intersection of tech and business. Its flagship **PGP TBM (Post Graduate Programme in Technology and Business Management)** is a 16-month MBA-equivalent that has built a strong reputation in the product, consulting, and tech-business roles market.

### Programs & Fees (2026)

| Program | Duration | Total Fees |
|---|---|---|
| PGP TBM (MBA-equivalent) | 16 months | ₹33.65 lakh |
| PGP TBM YLC (for freshers) | 24 months | ₹34 lakh |
| UG Program — India Track | 4 years | ₹52.50 lakh |
| UG Program — Global Track | 4 years | ₹114.80 lakh |

### Admissions

Selection is through **MU-BAAT** (Masters' Union Business Aptitude and Analytical Test), followed by a personal interview. The school evaluates work experience, academics, and communication. Acceptance rate sits at **12–15%** — genuinely selective.

### Placements (2024 Data)

- **Average Package:** ₹28.52 LPA
- **Highest Package:** ₹61.8 LPA
- Recruiters include product companies, consulting firms, and growth-stage startups

### Verdict on Masters' Union

If you have 2–5 years of work experience, want to pivot into a tech-adjacent role, and can back the ₹33+ lakh fee with a clear career thesis — Masters' Union is one of the strongest bets outside the IIM ecosystem. The placement average is hard to ignore. But it's not cheap, and it's not for everyone.

**Best fit:** Working professionals targeting product management, tech consulting, or business leadership roles at startups and mid-stage companies.

## Mesa School of Business: Built for Founders

**Location:** Adugodi, Bangalore
**Focus:** Entrepreneurship & Startup Leadership

Mesa is Bangalore's bet on a different question: what if B-school wasn't about getting a job, but about building one? The school has carved out a niche for students who want to start companies, join early-stage startups, or lead entrepreneurial functions inside larger organisations.

### Programs & Fees (2026)

| Program | Duration | Total Fees |
|---|---|---|
| UG in Business Management & Entrepreneurship | 4 years | ₹48 lakh |
| PGP in Startup Leadership (MSL) | 1 year | ₹17.45 lakh |

Mesa's PGP in Startup Leadership is notably the most affordable in this comparison at **₹17.45 lakh** — and the school offers scholarships ranging from **40% to 90%** of the programme fee based on student profile. That's a meaningful differentiator.

### Admissions

Selection is via **Mesa Admissions Test (MAT)** followed by a personal interview. The UG programme is open to candidates from any 10+2 stream. PGP requires a graduation degree and one year of preferred work experience.

### What Makes Mesa Different

Unlike Masters' Union which targets career pivots at established companies, Mesa's curriculum is built around venture creation and startup ecosystems. Think: business model design, cap table basics, fundraising, go-to-market strategy for early-stage companies — not just case studies about Fortune 500 decisions.

Bangalore as a campus city is also an underrated advantage. The startup density, VC proximity, and founder networks in Bangalore give Mesa students organic access to the ecosystem they're studying.

### Verdict on Mesa

If the IIM route doesn't align with your entrepreneurial goals and you don't want to spend ₹30+ lakh on a programme designed for corporate careers, Mesa's PGP at ₹17.45 lakh is genuinely compelling — especially with scholarship availability. But placement data is nascent, and the school is still building its alumni network and recruiter trust.

**Best fit:** Aspiring founders, early startup employees, and students who want a business education built around the startup ecosystem, not the corporate ladder.

## First Bridge: The AI-Native Business School

**Location:** Gurugram, Haryana
**Focus:** AI-integrated, Job-Ready Business Skills

First Bridge is the newest name in this comparison, and it has arrived with a sharp positioning: traditional MBA curricula are too slow, too theoretical, and too disconnected from how companies actually hire. First Bridge's PGP is designed around real hiring needs, with **80+ industry partners** shaping every module.

### Program Highlights

- **AI-integrated curriculum** — every domain (marketing, finance, operations, strategy) is taught through the lens of AI tools and data-driven decision-making. No coding knowledge required.
- **Portfolio-ready outputs** — each module ends with a deliverable: a dashboard, a strategy deck, a campaign plan, or a business model — not just an exam score.
- **CXO-heavy faculty** — founders, consultants, and senior professionals from IIMs and XLRI serve as faculty, rather than full-time academics.
- **100% placement assistance** built into the programme structure.

### Fees

First Bridge has not published a fixed fee card publicly — fees and scholarship structures are disclosed during the admissions process. Prospective students should contact the school directly or visit **firstbridge.ac** for current pricing.


### Verdict on First Bridge

First Bridge is the boldest conceptual bet of the three: that the future of business education is AI-native, output-oriented, and industry-assembled rather than academically designed. If that resonates with you, it's worth a serious look. The absence of public fee disclosure is a yellow flag — but the industry partnership model and AI integration are genuinely ahead of the curve.

**Best fit:** Students who want a practical, fast-track business education with strong AI skilling and don't want a 2-year traditional MBA, but need more transparency on fee structure before deciding.

## Are They Worth It? The ROI Question

Here's how to think about it honestly:

**Masters' Union at ₹33.65 lakh** with a ₹28.52 LPA average placement means a rough payback period of about 14–16 months post-joining, assuming competitive savings. That math works — if you land a median or above placement.

**Mesa's PGP at ₹17.45 lakh** (or less with a scholarship) is the lowest financial risk in this comparison. But the career outcome depends heavily on your own founder ambition — there's no traditional corporate placement guarantee here.

**First Bridge** — until fees are public and a placement track record builds, it's a conviction bet on a genuinely innovative model. Do your due diligence.

## Frequently Asked Questions

> **Are Masters' Union, Mesa, and First Bridge UGC-recognised?**
These are private institutions operating under various legal structures. Always verify accreditation, degree equivalence, and recognition status directly with the school before enrolling. UGC recognition status determines how your degree is treated by employers and for further studies.

> **Do these schools accept CAT scores?**
No. All three run their own admission tests — MU-BAAT (Masters' Union), Mesa Admissions Test (Mesa), and their own screening (First Bridge). CAT/XAT scores are not required.

> **Can freshers apply?**
Masters' Union has a specific YLC cohort for freshers (24 months). Mesa's PGP prefers one year of work experience but it's not mandatory. First Bridge accepts freshers with strong profiles.

> **Which school has the best placement record?**
Masters' Union has the most established and publicly reported placement data (₹28.52 LPA average, 2024). Mesa and First Bridge are younger institutions still building their track record.

> **How do these compare to a Tier-2 IIM?**
Tier-2 IIMs carry stronger brand recognition with traditional recruiters. These new-age schools are better suited for students targeting startup ecosystems, product roles, and non-traditional career paths.

## Final Verdict

There's no single winner here — because these three schools are playing different games.

Choose **Masters' Union** if you have work experience, a clear tech-adjacent career goal, and can justify ₹33+ lakh with your target role's salary band.

Choose **Mesa** if entrepreneurship is your north star and you want India's most affordable new-age B-school with genuine startup DNA.

Choose **First Bridge** if you believe AI-native business education is the future and you're comfortable doing deep due diligence on a newer institution.

What all three share: they're built for a student who knows what they want. If you're still figuring that out, clarify your career goals first — then pick your school.
