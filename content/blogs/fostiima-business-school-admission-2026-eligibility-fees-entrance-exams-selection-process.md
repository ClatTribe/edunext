## About FOSTIIMA Business School

FOSTIIMA Business School was established in **2007** and is located at **Plot No. HAF-1, Dwarka Sector 9, New Delhi**. The institute is **AICTE-approved** and offers the **Post Graduate Diploma in Management (PGDM)** — a 2-year full-time programme recognised as equivalent to an MBA by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU).

The school offers two variants of the PGDM:

- **PGDM (Regular)** — open to all eligible candidates
- **PGDM (Select Group)** — a merit-tracked cohort for high-scoring CAT/XAT candidates, with additional scholarship benefits

Both are 2-year, full-time programmes delivered from the Dwarka campus.

## Entrance Exams Accepted (2026)

FOSTIIMA accepts scores from all major national management entrance exams — plus its own in-house test for candidates who haven't appeared in any national exam.

| Exam | Type | Conducted by |
|---|---|---|
| CAT | Computer-based national test | IIMs (IIM Calcutta for 2026) |
| XAT | Computer-based national test | XLRI Jamshedpur |
| CMAT | Computer-based national test | NTA |
| MAT | Paper-based / Computer-based | AIMA |
| GMAT | Computer-based international test | GMAC |
| **FEAT** | FOSTIIMA's own aptitude test | FOSTIIMA Business School |

### What Is FEAT?

**FEAT (FOSTIIMA Eligibility and Aptitude Test)** is FOSTIIMA's in-house entrance test, designed specifically for candidates who have not appeared in CAT, XAT, CMAT, or any other national-level management test. It tests quantitative ability, verbal reasoning, and data interpretation — broadly similar in format to CMAT but administered directly by the school.

If you've already taken CAT/XAT/CMAT/GMAT/MAT, you **do not need to appear for FEAT** — your national test score qualifies you directly for the GD and PI rounds.

## Step-by-Step Selection Process (2026)

FOSTIIMA's admission process runs in four stages:

### Stage 1: Application

Submit an online or offline application form with academic documents, entrance test scorecard, and the application fee. Applications for the 2026–28 batch are processed on a rolling basis — early applications receive priority shortlisting.

### Stage 2: Shortlisting

Shortlisting is based on a composite score that includes:

- Entrance exam score (CAT / XAT / CMAT / GMAT / MAT / FEAT)
- Academic performance in 10th, 12th, and graduation
- Work experience (bonus weightage, not mandatory)

There is no disclosed minimum percentile cutoff for national exams — the shortlisting is merit-based relative to the applicant pool for each batch.

### Stage 3: Group Discussion (GD)

Shortlisted candidates are called for a Group Discussion round. GD topics are typically current affairs, business case scenarios, or management-themed discussions. Communication clarity, logical reasoning, and collaborative behaviour are evaluated.

### Stage 4: Personal Interview (PI)

The PI assesses motivation for management education, career goals, subject knowledge, and overall personality fit. Candidates with prior work experience are expected to speak concretely about their professional contributions. Freshers are assessed on academic depth and clarity of intent.

### Final Selection

Final merit is calculated using:

| Component | Weightage |
|---|---|
| Entrance Exam Score | Primary |
| Academic Record (10th + 12th + Grad) | Secondary |
| GD Performance | Secondary |
| PI Performance | Secondary |
| Work Experience | Bonus |

Offer letters are issued on a rolling basis — seats fill across rounds, so applying early materially improves your chances.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the minimum CAT percentile required for FOSTIIMA admission?**
FOSTIIMA does not publish a fixed minimum CAT percentile cutoff. Admission is merit-based within the applicant pool. However, based on general trends, candidates with 50–70 percentile in CAT/CMAT are competitive applicants. Higher scores qualify for Select Group scholarships.

**Is the FOSTIIMA PGDM equivalent to an MBA degree?**
Yes. The PGDM offered by FOSTIIMA is recognised as equivalent to an MBA by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU). It is AICTE-approved. However, it is not a university degree — it is a diploma. Verify equivalence requirements with specific employers or higher education institutions if needed.

**Can I apply to FOSTIIMA without appearing in CAT or any national exam?**
Yes. FOSTIIMA's own FEAT (FOSTIIMA Eligibility and Aptitude Test) is available for candidates who have not taken CAT, XAT, CMAT, GMAT, or MAT. A valid FEAT score qualifies candidates for the GD and PI rounds.

**Does FOSTIIMA accept applications from final-year students?**
Yes. Final-year graduation students can apply provisionally. Admission is confirmed upon submission of the final marksheet with the minimum 50% aggregate requirement met.

**When do FOSTIIMA 2026 admissions close?**
FOSTIIMA processes admissions on a rolling basis across multiple rounds. Applications typically remain open from November through June for the July intake. Visit [fostiima.org](https://fostiima.org) or [fostiima.org/admissions/how-to-apply.php](https://fostiima.org/admissions/how-to-apply.php) for the current 2026 round schedule.
