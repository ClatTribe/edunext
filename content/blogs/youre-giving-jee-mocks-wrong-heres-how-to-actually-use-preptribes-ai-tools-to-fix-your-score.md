## Why More Mocks Without Analysis Is Making You Worse

Here's the truth coaching teachers rarely say out loud: giving mock tests without deep analysis doesn't just fail to help — it can actively hurt you.

Every time you practise a mistake without correcting it, you reinforce that mistake. Your brain gets better at making the same wrong move faster. Twenty mocks of poor analysis builds twenty layers of bad habits.

One properly dissected mock test is worth more than five rushed ones. The question is — what does real analysis actually look like?

After every full-length JEE mock, you need answers to these questions:

- Which subject cost me the most marks — and *why*?
- Which chapters are appearing in my wrong answers repeatedly?
- Which questions should I have skipped but didn't?
- Am I actually improving across mocks, or just fluctuating?
- What should I study *tomorrow* based on everything above?

## Two Training Modes That Fix What Mocks Reveal

Analysis tells you what went wrong. These two PrepTribe modes fix it.

**Sudden Death** — One wrong answer ends the session. No retries. This rewires the most important JEE habit: slowing down enough to be accurate before being fast. Students whose Meri Report flags careless mistakes and accuracy errors as a core problem should use this daily. Most see a measurable drop in silly errors within two weeks.

**Skip or Solve** — A question appears with a countdown timer. Decide instantly: commit and solve, or skip and move on. This builds the snap-judgement instinct that top JEE scorers have — knowing in 15 seconds whether a question is worth your time. If your mock analysis shows consistent time management failure, this is your fix.
