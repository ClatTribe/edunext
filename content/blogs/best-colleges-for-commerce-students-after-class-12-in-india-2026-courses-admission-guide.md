# Best colleges for commerce students after Class 12 in India 2026: courses & admission guide

## Quick summary

- Commerce after Class 12 opens five main paths: **B.Com, BBA, CA/CS/CMA, Economics (Hons), and integrated law or management (IPM)**. Each leads to genuinely different careers.
- The best colleges for commerce — SRCC Delhi, NLSIU (integrated law), IIM Indore IPM, Symbiosis, Christ University — each have different entrance exams and timelines. One strategy does not fit all.
- Since CUET replaced board-percentage admissions at all central universities in 2022, scoring 90%+ in boards no longer guarantees a SRCC or DU seat. Your CUET score in Economics, Accountancy, and Business Studies decides it.

## The five main paths for commerce students after Class 12

### 1. B.Com and B.Com (Hons)

**Best route:** CUET 2026 (for central universities like DU, BHU, JNU, Jamia) or university-specific entrance tests.

**What it leads to:** CA, CS, MBA, investment banking, financial analysis, corporate finance, and civil services.

### 4. Chartered Accountancy (CA)

CA is not a college course — it is a professional certification run by ICAI (Institute of Chartered Accountants of India). There is no entrance exam and no college to join. You register directly with ICAI after Class 12 and pursue Foundation → Intermediate → Final while doing articleship.

**What makes it different:** CA can be done alongside a B.Com degree (many students do both). The CA designation, once earned, is one of the highest-paying credentials in Indian finance without requiring an MBA.

**Key facts for 2026:**
- ICAI CA Foundation registration is open year-round
- Foundation exam appears twice a year (January and July)
- Articleship (3 years of practical training) is mandatory before Final
- Pass rate is low — roughly 25–35% at Foundation, 10–15% at Final. It requires consistent effort over 4–5 years.

**Also available:** CS (Company Secretary via ICSI) and CMA (Cost Management Accountancy via ICMAI) follow similar structures and are strong alternative professional qualifications.

## The CUET factor: what changed for commerce admissions in 2026

Before 2022, getting into SRCC or Hindu College meant scoring 98%+ in boards. That era ended when UGC mandated CUET for all 45 central universities. Board marks now only establish eligibility (you must have passed Class 12 with Commerce subjects). Your CUET score in the relevant domain papers determines your merit rank.

For B.Com (Hons) at DU, the relevant CUET papers are: **Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics** (domain subjects) + **English** (language paper). Your combined performance in these four papers, calculated by DU's formula under CSAS, determines your rank across all DU B.Com colleges.

A student with 78% in boards and 188/200 in CUET domain papers will rank above a student with 96% in boards and 165/200 in CUET. This is not a hypothetical — it is how DU admissions have worked since 2022.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which is better after Class 12 commerce — B.Com or BBA?

B.Com (Hons) from SRCC or Hindu College is academically stronger and cheaper. BBA from IIM Indore IPM gives you an IIM pedigree and management focus. If your goal is an MBA, BBA from a top institution gives you a head start. If your goal is CA, finance, or civil services, B.Com is more aligned. Both are strong choices — the college matters more than the course name.

### Can a commerce student get into IIM through IPM?

Yes. IIM Indore, IIM Rohtak, and IIM Ranchi offer Integrated Programme in Management (IPM) — a 5-year BBA + MBA that admits students directly after Class 12 through IPMAT. It is the most direct route from Class 12 to an IIM degree, and it is open to all streams (not just commerce).

### What CUET score do I need for SRCC B.Com (Hons)?

Based on 2025 CSAS data, SRCC B.Com (Hons) in General category typically requires 185–195/200 in domain subjects and 180+ in English. The exact cutoff shifts yearly based on the applicant pool. Check EduNext's DU cutoff tracker for category-wise data.

### Is CA better than B.Com from a top college?

They are not substitutes — they are complementary. CA is a professional certification; B.Com is an academic degree. Many students do both simultaneously. A CA + B.Com from a decent college is a stronger combination than either alone. If you can handle the workload, pursuing both in parallel is the standard approach among serious finance aspirants.

### Do commerce students need to take CUET even for private colleges?

Not for most private colleges — each has its own entrance exam or merit-based admission. CUET is mandatory only for central universities (DU, JNU, BHU, Jamia, and the other 41 central universities). Top private colleges like NMIMS (NPAT), Symbiosis (SET), and Christ University have separate processes.
